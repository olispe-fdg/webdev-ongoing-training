1979, Recursive ray tracing

Ray tracing as we know it today was pioneered by Turner Whitted in his 1979 paper An Improved Illumination Model for Shaded Display. While similar algorithms in the past had achieved some form of ray tracing, Whitted introduced recursion for tracing reflection and refraction which separated ray tracing from other rendering methods at the time.
